ESG Approach

TECOM Group’s robust governance & ESG framework are at the core of our operating model. Our holistic approach to ESG is centred around TECOM’s five ESG pillars:

As a key player in one of the largest business segments in the UAE, TECOM Group is also committed to supporting a number of national mandates including: UAE Centennial 2071 [insert link] Dubai’s Clean Energy Strategy [insert link] Dubai Integrated Energy Strategy 2030 [insert link, please note, no direct link to strategy could be found] UAE Net Zero Emissions Strategy 2050 [insert link] UAE Green Growth Strategy 2015-2030 [insert link]

Our Group-wide ESG strategic initiatives are aligned with the United Nation’s Sustainable Development Goals and 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. They include:

  • Solid ESG framework based on insights from internal and external stakeholders through our 2022 Materiality Assessment
  • Award-winning health, safety and environmental governance model with annual and measurable long-term environmental goals, emissions targets and sustainability initiatives
  • Energy audits and the implementation of a portfolio-wide utility monitoring and analytics platform to monitor energy and water consumption
  • Projects for reducing energy consumption and operational carbon footprint, as well as investments in low-carbon energy sources
  • Group-wide environmental policy and strict operating guidelines to limit the impact on the land and the surrounding areas in which we operate
  • Sector-focused community investment programmes and policies as well as volunteering efforts
  • Incubators and support for start-ups, SMEs and freelancers to nurture talent and innovation in Dubai
  • Experienced Board of Directors and an effective and transparent governance framework in line with international standards and regulatory requirements
  • Additional non-mandatory Risk Committee that oversees ESG matters to ensure TECOM fulfils its responsibilities towards our stakeholders
  • Transparent annual reporting on ESG as per the ESG reporting guidelines of the Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA)

Key TECOM Group ESG Achievements
  • Investments in five onsite solar energy projects with 7.8 megawatts peak (MWP) capacity across several business districts.
  • 12.1 GW hours of capacity generation from solar energy
  • Dubai Outsource City Solar Carport was activated in June 2020 and annual savings achieved reached 100%*
  • Dubai International Academic City carport was activated in September 2020 and annual savings achieved reached 22%*
  • Dubai International Academic City Rooftop was activated in December 2021 and annual savings is set to reach 16%*
  • Workers’ accommodation (LV1) was activated in November 2021 and annual savings is set to reach 28%*
  • Dubai Production City units were activated in March 2022 and annual savings is set to reach 100%*
  • 9% of buildings have Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) certification, (16 LEED Gold and 6 LEED Silver)
  • 46 different nationalities represented in the workforce with a 24% Emiratisation rate
  • 32% female representation in the workforce and 57% female new hires as of December 2021
  • 0 work-related injuries or fatalities since 2019
  • Investment in platforms and services to nurture innovation provide tailored support for start-ups, SMEs, and freelancers such as in5, axs, GoFreelance, marketplace and D/Quarters.
  • 4,600+ beneficiaries of Corporate Social Responsibility activities since 2019

*Saving from TECOM Group's electricity accounts

Selected TECOM ESG Goals
Alignment of Group-wide ESG practices with global standards such as the Global Real Estate Sustainability Benchmark (GRESB).
Continued diversity and inclusion initiatives to increase female representation in overall workforce and a 30% female representation in senior and middle management by 2025.
Planned retrofitting projects resulting in expected electricity savings of 35,833,180 kilowatt hours (kWH) and water savings of 69,967 cubic meters versus 2019 utility consumption baseline.
Additional 1.54 megawatts peak (MWP) capacity from solar energy sources by the end of 2022.
Further solar carport projects in several districts to offset additional CO2.
Monitoring and reporting on scope 2 and 3 greenhouse gases in accordance with the Greenhouse Gas Protocol.
